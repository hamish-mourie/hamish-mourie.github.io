Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people:

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity boost peoples’ capabilities in learning. By changing a behaviour peoples’ brain cells change in response to that. By frequently engaging in that behaviour the connections between the brain cells are physically strengthened.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit:

Reminding myself that my capabilities are malleable through the means of continuous and gradual behavioural actions.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?:

I can select an activity to undertake that is new, challenging and important to me. I can enhance my ability in programming by tapping into my existing neural pathways. By prioritising activities which are already routine (such as gaming) I can integrate aspects of programming into them to enhance my neuroplasticity.

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant:

A growth mindset refers to the belief that talents can be developed through hard work, good strategies, and continuous practice. A fixed mindset on the other hand refers to the belief that talents are innate and cannot be improved. Having a growth mindset is relevant because it creates opportunities to learn, gain new skills, and enhance existing skills.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?:

No, I’m familiar with this concept as whilst studying for my degree in psychology it came up. I already hold the belief that if I haven’t learnt something it’s only because I haven’t attempted to do so.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?:

Even if something looks incredibly complex at first glance it can be broken down into smaller components and eventually be achieved over a long period of time and effort.
