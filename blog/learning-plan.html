Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal is to work in IT due to the possibility of working remotely. After obtaining several years of experience a career working as a full-stack developer appeals to me. I hope to use the skills gained from Dev Academy to be able to develop web applications.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My strengths and limitations mentioned below are still valid. For bootcamp I think my biggest strength will be the ability to meet deadlines and expectations, as I dedicate extra time to things to ensure that they get finished to a standard I’m proud of. I think that the biggest limitation I’ll face during bootcamp is the tendency to not reach out to others to find out how they’re going with things.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

The biggest non-technical challenge I’ll face for bootcamp initially will be the 8.30am-5.30pm schedule, as I normally do work in the afternoon until late. I think I’ll also find it a challenge to be constantly present in video calls, as I’m accustomed to working independently.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I’d like to develop the mindset to work on projects which go beyond just deadlines and expectations (as in, attempting stretch activities which go beyond what’s expected).

I’d also like to develop the ability to reach out to others to find out how they’re progressing with the course.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

To respond to reasonable student enquiries to the best of their abilities during standard working hours.

In regards to technical aspects of the course I currently have the expectation that learning will be more teacher driven, rather than independently driven like it was in foundations.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

My personal expectation is that I'll be online actively learning during 8.30am-5.30pm, Monday-Friday; and if needed an additional 2 hours at night could be spent solidifying learning content. I will seek help firstly from my peers, then secondly from the bootcamp team. I’ll seek help if stuck on a single problem for more than 30 minutes.

To work with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives I will apply skills which are conducive to productive and safe communication which follow core values relating to diversity, empathy, wellbeing, and trust.

Foundations Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

To work as a full-stack developer.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning:

Strengths: When others are reliant upon me learning something, then I will ensure that I understand/complete the learning content.

Limitations: Without deadlines or others’ expectations I often don’t engage with learning content in a timely manner.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I’d like to develop the ability to reach out to others.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way:

If I cannot work something out myself within 30 minutes then I will seek help from my peers. If my peers cannot help me within a few hours then I will get help from a Dev Academy facilitator.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team:

To respond to reasonable student enquiries to the best of their abilities during standard working hours.

Any scheduling information:

I have no scheduled commitments to others that would impact my learning at Dev Academy.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme:

To work productively I’ll set goals at the start of every day and manage my time appropriately.

To work productively and safely with others involved in the Dev Academy programme I’ll aim to implement actions and behaviours that align with the core values of integrity, kindness and effort.

When the workload builds up and starts getting to me I’ll take a step back and look at the bigger picture. When looking at the bigger picture I can often see that a few things have been taking up my attention for too long, and that I should move on to the next thing to continue progressing.
